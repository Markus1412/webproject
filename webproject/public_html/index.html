Make Ruter great again

Help improve our public transport system by reporting and discussing problems in the new RuterFeedback update.

Join the forum

You can comment on current issues or make your own new topic whenever you see something you want improved or fixed.

Share your opinion

The app allows the user to share their opinions with their families and friends through different social medias. Let people know what you think!

Problem

- Vandalism on public transport does not get the attention it needs.

- There are plenty of vehicles that have obvious flaws, and if it got more attention from the public it would get sorted out faster.

- We think the problem exists partly because notifying Ruter is a hassle, because of an old and outdated system.

- Currently you need to call them to report a problem, and most people would rather not report it at all then.

- It might be hard to know how you can report a potential case of vandalism, and Ruter could do a slightly better job at letting people know how they can help.

Solution

- The solution to the vandalism problem is to create an app-extension to the RuterReise app.

- The main objective of this app is to get people to report vandalism, and it would be important for the app to be easy to use to avoid people getting too annoyed reporting a problem.

- A user could use the feedback function during a ride or after getting off. There would be no restriction to giving feedback.

- This solution should increase willingness to participate and report cases of vandalism, especially from younger audiences.

Potential problems

- Since the replies will be available to see for everyone, there is a possibility for spam. To avoid this we could implement a GPS-restriction that would only allow a customer to give feedback if they are (or were) in the vehicle in the past 24h.

- The app(like any app, not this in particular) might be too technical for older people, but it wouldn’t be an issue assuming they kept the option to call to report a problem.

Extra benefits

- People could automatically get involved/aware by getting a notification once the RuterReise app has been updated with the Ruterfeedback function. This would be a one-time thing, but it would make sure people are aware of the new possibilities within the app.

- Having an app like this can generate different statistics that might be valuable for Ruter.

- Because the app is included in the RuterReise app customers would not have to deal with extra clutter on their phones. Ideally the app-extension would be lightweight.

- The app will work as a base platform for Ruter, and they could expand it easily.

- The app could be expanded to include feedback on drivers/general routes. This could be hidden feedback to avoid spam and hopefully only generate proper responses.

  • Choose Vehicle

    The user can access this new function by opening the RuterReiser app, then selecting RuterFeedback. Doing this will take the user to this selection page where you can choose from 7 different vehicles. You pick whichever vehicle you used/are using and wish to provide feedback for.

  • Select your Metro

    In this example the user selected Metro on the previous screen. The next step is to pick the correct Metro to make sure you are giving feedback in the proper forum.

    By default the app shows nearby vehicles and lets you select the one you are currently on. If you are giving feedback after stepping off the Metro you can change the nearby option to recent or history. The RuterReiser app already has GPS tracking for other functions and will work for RuterFeedback to ensure you are sent to the forum for the vehicle you were traveling with.

    You also have the option to search for a specific Metro if you can’t find the one you are on/recently used.

    The user does not need to know the specific vehicle-number, just the route. The specific vehicle number will be worked out through the Ruter database using GPS and your travel history.

  • Feedback Forum

    After selecting your Metro you get sent to the feedback forum for that specific vehicle. Here you can see current topics and decide to read/reply to them if you wish. You can also give them a thumbs up/down or use the share button to spread this issue to social media. The user can sort the current topics by likes, comments or date.

    A user can easily make a new topic by clicking on the “Feedback…” box. This box will then expand and allow the user to write down their feedback.

    In this specific example picture the user selected Bus -> Route 32 to get sent to this feedback forum.
